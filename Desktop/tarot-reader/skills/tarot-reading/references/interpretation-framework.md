# Interpretation Framework

This framework guides how to synthesize card meanings into a coherent, contextually relevant reading. Apply these principles after loading the relevant card references.

---

## Core Philosophy

**1. Context shapes meaning**
Traditional card meanings are starting points — the question context determines which facet of a card's energy is most alive. The Tower in a career question speaks to professional upheaval; in a relationship question, it speaks to relational disruption. The suit, number, and archetype provide the range; the question specifies the register.

**2. Readings are conversations, not verdicts**
Every reading describes energies and trajectories, not fixed outcomes. The cards show what is present — the querent decides what to do with it. Language should always preserve agency: "this energy suggests" rather than "this will happen."

**3. Emotional resonance belongs in the reading**
After delivering the core interpretation, invite the querent to notice which cards draw them in or repel them. Unexpected reactions (finding comfort in a "difficult" card, feeling unsettled by a "positive" one) often hold significant information. Reflect these reactions back as data.

**4. One actionable takeaway per reading**
Every reading should end with one concrete, specific thing the querent can notice, do, or shift in perspective within the next few days. Abstract wisdom is less useful than practical orientation.

## Mode-Specific Interpretive Lenses

### 复盘过去 (Past Review)

Primary questions to answer through the cards:
- What actually happened — what were the forces at play?
- Why did it unfold this way — what patterns, beliefs, or circumstances drove it?
- What is the central lesson or insight this experience is offering?
- What can be released now, and what should be integrated?

**Language frame:** Causality, patterns, completion. What has been learned. What is being carried forward that should be set down.

**Tone:** Compassionate and honest — not self-critical, not defensive. The past is information, not judgment.

**Special considerations:**
- Major Arcana in past review = archetypal forces were at work, not just circumstance
- Swords in past review often = intellectual or communicative patterns that shaped events
- Reversed cards in past review = energies that were operating below awareness, unacknowledged

---

### 校正当下 (Present Calibration)

Primary questions to answer through the cards:
- What is the current energy of the situation — what is actually happening beneath the surface?
- What is in alignment (working well) and what is out of alignment (creating friction)?
- What is the one most significant adjustment that would shift the most?
- What direction is the energy already naturally moving, and how to work with it rather than against it?

**Language frame:** Current state, micro-adjustments, recalibration. Small shifts with outsized effects. Direction rather than destination.

**Tone:** Practical and clear — the present moment is workable; the reading helps the querent orient within it.

**Special considerations:**
- Cups dominant = emotional landscape needs attention; the inner state is the calibration point
- Wands dominant = energy and motivation are the calibration point; are they directed or scattered?
- Pentacles dominant = practical/material circumstances need examination
- The tension card (the one that doesn't fit) often marks what is most out of alignment

---

### 预测未来 (Future Prediction)

Primary questions to answer through the cards:
- What trajectory is being set in motion by current conditions?
- What hidden factors or influences will shape how things develop?
- What would help navigate this trajectory most effectively?
- What is the most likely outcome if current course is maintained?

**Language frame:** Conditional and trajectory-based. "If current conditions hold, then..." / "The energy suggests movement toward..." — never deterministic.

**Tone:** Honest about challenges, clear about possibilities, emphasizing preparation and navigation.

**Special considerations:**
- Future readings are most useful when framed as "what to prepare for" not "what will definitely happen"
- When a difficult card appears in the outcome position: name it clearly, explain what it means, then address what conditions would shift it
- Timing: Wands = fast; Cups = variable, emotionally driven; Swords = quicker, sharper turns; Pentacles = slow, seasonal

## Card Combination Rules

### Major + Major
When multiple Major Arcana appear together, **archetypal forces are dominant**. The reading carries larger-than-personal significance — this is not just about the immediate situation but about deeper soul-level patterns or life-stage transitions. Treat each major as equally weighty; the interaction between them is the key insight.

### Major + Minor
The Major Arcana **sets the theme**; the Minor Arcana provides **practical texture**. The major anchors the spiritual/archetypal context; the minor describes how that context plays out in day-to-day life. E.g., The Hermit (IX) + Eight of Pentacles = a period of solitude that deepens professional skill and craft.

### Minor + Minor
A **pragmatic, grounded reading** focused on practical circumstances and day-to-day energy. No major archetypal forces — this is about actions, relationships, and material conditions right now. The suits' interaction guides interpretation: same suit = amplification; opposing suits (Fire/Water, Air/Earth) = tension or balancing.

### Reversed Cards
Reversed cards indicate energy that is:
- **Internalized**: The card's themes are being processed within rather than expressed outward
- **Delayed**: The energy is present but blocked or not yet fully activated
- **Shadow expression**: The challenging or unconscious face of the card's archetype is more prominent
- **Not inherently negative**: A reversed "difficult" card (like Death or The Tower) can indicate slower or gentler transformation

Do not treat reversals as simply "bad" — they add nuance, not negativity. If the querent didn't specify whether cards were upright or reversed, ask only if it would meaningfully change the reading.

## Synthesis Method

To move from individual card meanings to a coherent reading, apply these steps in sequence:

### 1. Identify the Dominant Energy
Survey all cards for:
- **Majority suit**: If 3 of 4 cards are Cups, the emotional domain is dominant
- **Major vs. Minor balance**: More Majors = archetypal/life-stage focus; more Minors = practical/day-to-day focus
- **Number patterns**: Multiple high numbers (8-10) suggest completion energy; multiple low numbers suggest beginnings

### 2. Find the Tension Card
Identify which card stands apart from the others — the one that introduces a different energy, element, or directional pull. This card is usually the most important: it reveals the complication, the hidden factor, or the key question the reading is really answering.

### 3. Build the Narrative Arc
In sequence across positions:
- **Where did this begin / what is the foundation?** (first card or earliest position)
- **Where is this now / what is in motion?** (middle position)
- **Where is this heading / what is being built toward?** (final position)

Connect these into a flowing narrative sentence before elaborating each card's specific meaning. This arc prevents the reading from feeling like a list of disconnected card definitions.

### 4. Translate to the Question Domain
Return to the querent's specific question and filter the narrative through it:
- Career question: translate all cards' energies into professional terms
- Relationship question: translate through relational dynamics and emotional patterns
- Inner/psychological question: translate through beliefs, self-perception, and emotional processes
- Decision question: position the cards as perspectives on the options, not predictions of fixed outcomes

### 5. Honor Complexity
If the cards present contradictory energies (e.g., The Sun alongside The Nine of Swords), name the contradiction directly. Real situations are often contradictory — the reading's job is to map it accurately, not to smooth it into false simplicity.

## Delivery Principles

**Do say:**
- "This suggests..." / "The cards point toward..."
- "If [condition], then [possibility]..."
- "This pattern often reflects..."
- "Notice if this resonates..."
- "The tension here is between..."

**Avoid:**
- "This will happen" / "This means you will..."
- "This is definitely..." / "The cards show that..."
- "This is a bad sign" without accompanying context
- Reducing a complex card to one-word meaning

**When a difficult card appears (Death, The Tower, Ten of Swords, etc.):**
Name it clearly, explain what it actually means (not the surface fear), acknowledge the difficulty if present, then address what it opens up or what navigation looks like. Do not soft-pedal difficult cards — the querent is asking because they need honest insight.

**On timing questions:**
Tarot does not give precise dates. Offer general timing cues:
- Wands: fast, within weeks to a few months
- Cups: variable, driven by emotional readiness
- Swords: quicker, sharp turning points
- Pentacles: slow, seasonal, months to over a year
- Major Arcana: timing is less relevant than preparation and awareness
