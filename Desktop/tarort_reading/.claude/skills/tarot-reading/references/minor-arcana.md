# Minor Arcana Reference

The 56 Minor Arcana address day-to-day life, practical circumstances, and the texture of lived experience. They are divided into four suits, each governing a domain:

- **Wands (权杖)** — Fire element: ambition, passion, creativity, action, career drive, life force
- **Cups (圣杯)** — Water element: emotion, relationships, intuition, imagination, inner life
- **Swords (宝剑)** — Air element: thought, communication, conflict, clarity, decision, truth
- **Pentacles (星币 / 钱币)** — Earth element: material world, work, body, finances, practical matters, long-term building

**Number meanings (across suits):**
- Ace: Pure potential, seed energy, new beginning in that suit's domain
- 2: Balance, choice, early partnership, tension between two paths
- 3: Initial growth, early celebration or conflict, first tangible results
- 4: Consolidation, rest, pause, assessment
- 5: Conflict, loss, instability, challenge — but also necessary disruption
- 6: Harmony, generosity, exchange, recovering balance
- 7: Assessment, reflection, inner work, possible deception or self-examination
- 8: Movement, skill development, rapid change, focused work
- 9: Near-completion, resilience, culmination of lessons
- 10: Completion, fulfillment or overload, cycle's end
- Page: New energy, student/messenger, youthful curiosity in that domain
- Knight: Active pursuit, movement, the energy of that suit in action
- Queen: Mastery from within, emotional intelligence applied to that suit's domain
- King: Outward mastery, authority, the mature expression of that suit

## Suit of Wands (权杖 / 火)

*Domain: ambition, passion, creativity, career drive, life force, inspiration*

### Ace of Wands (权杖王牌)
**Upright:** A surge of creative or professional inspiration — the spark before the fire. New opportunity, fresh motivation, the impulse to begin something meaningful. Act on this energy before it dissipates.
**Reversed:** Creative block, false start, lack of follow-through on an idea. Energy is present but directionless or suppressed.

### Two of Wands (权杖二)
**Upright:** Standing at the threshold with the world in hand — planning a significant move, weighing possibilities, early ambition taking shape. Personal power being directed toward a chosen vision.
**Reversed:** Fear of stepping forward, indecision, staying within the familiar when the horizon calls.

### Three of Wands (权杖三)
**Upright:** The first ships have gone out — early ventures are underway, awaiting results. Expanded thinking, looking toward the larger horizon, confidence in what was set in motion.
**Reversed:** Delays in plans, obstacles to expansion, impatience with the time required for things to develop.

### Four of Wands (权杖四)
**Upright:** Celebration, homecoming, a moment of genuine stability and joy. Community, milestone reached, a foundation to stand on and enjoy. Often appears around happy events, completions, or a settling into belonging.
**Reversed:** Instability in home or relationships despite external appearance of celebration. Incomplete joy, disrupted harmony.

### Five of Wands (权杖五)
**Upright:** Competition, conflict, scattered energy, multiple voices or priorities all clamoring at once. Can be productive creative tension or frustrating interference depending on context.
**Reversed:** Avoiding conflict that needs to happen, internal struggle, competition turned inward.

### Six of Wands (权杖六)
**Upright:** Victory, public recognition, success acknowledged. The effort paid off and others can see it. Confidence earned through achievement.
**Reversed:** Delayed victory, needing external validation, arrogance, or success that is hollow.

### Seven of Wands (权杖七)
**Upright:** Holding ground against opposition. Others may challenge the position, but the advantage is real — this is about defending what has been built with conviction and stamina.
**Reversed:** Overwhelmed by opposition, giving up ground too easily, self-doubt eroding a defensible position.

### Eight of Wands (权杖八)
**Upright:** Rapid movement, swift action, messages arriving, things accelerating. After a period of waiting, things are finally in motion — keep up. Travel, communication, or momentum.
**Reversed:** Delays after momentum was expected, miscommunication, energy moving in multiple contradictory directions.

### Nine of Wands (权杖九)
**Upright:** Battle-weary but still standing. The querent has been through difficulty and bears the marks of it, but resilience remains. One more push is needed; the goal is closer than it feels.
**Reversed:** Exhaustion from prolonged struggle, stubbornness that has become self-defeating, paranoia about threats that may not be real.

### Ten of Wands (权杖十)
**Upright:** Carrying too much. Success has come, but the burden of it is heavy — responsibilities, commitments, or obligations that have piled up. Something needs to be put down.
**Reversed:** Refusing to delegate or release burden, collapse under pressure, or finally letting go of what was too heavy.

### Page of Wands (权杖侍者)
**Upright:** Enthusiastic messenger or young energy full of creative ideas. News about projects or passions. A fresh, exploratory approach — curious, eager, not yet tested.
**Reversed:** Impulsive, unreliable, all spark and no follow-through. Ideas without execution.

### Knight of Wands (权杖骑士)
**Upright:** Bold, passionate forward motion. Taking action on ambition with speed and confidence. Adventurous and inspiring, though sometimes reckless.
**Reversed:** Hot-headed impulsiveness, rushing without strategy, scattered energy, instability.

### Queen of Wands (权杖王后)
**Upright:** Magnetic confidence, warmth, creative authority. Someone who knows what she wants and attracts others to her vision. Independent, passionate, deeply comfortable in her own power.
**Reversed:** Jealousy, manipulation, self-centeredness, or insecurity masquerading as confidence.

### King of Wands (权杖国王)
**Upright:** Bold visionary leadership. The entrepreneur, the creative director, the one who sees the big picture and inspires others to build it. Acts from conviction rather than process.
**Reversed:** Domineering, impractical, all vision with no grounding, or using charisma for manipulation.

## Suit of Cups (圣杯 / 水)

*Domain: emotion, relationships, intuition, imagination, dreams, the inner life*

### Ace of Cups (圣杯王牌)
**Upright:** Emotional new beginning — love offered or received, intuitive opening, creative or spiritual inspiration arising from feeling. The heart overflows.
**Reversed:** Emotional repression, blocked feeling, an opportunity for connection that isn't being received or recognized.

### Two of Cups (圣杯二)
**Upright:** Deep mutual connection — romantic or otherwise. Two people recognizing something in each other, a partnership of equals, a genuine bond beginning or deepening.
**Reversed:** Disconnection, imbalance in a relationship, or a partnership that looks connected but isn't aligned at a deeper level.

### Three of Cups (圣杯三)
**Upright:** Friendship, celebration, community joy. Coming together with others to share good things — a party, a reunion, the pleasure of belonging to a circle.
**Reversed:** Isolation, overindulgence, superficial socializing, or gossip within a group.

### Four of Cups (圣杯四)
**Upright:** Apathy, withdrawal, contemplation that has become disengagement. A hand offers a cup from a cloud, but the figure looks away — there may be more on offer than is being seen. Often appears when the querent feels bored, disconnected, or stuck.
**Reversed:** Emerging from withdrawal, finally reaching for what is being offered, renewed motivation.

### Five of Cups (圣杯五)
**Upright:** Grief, loss, regret. Three cups are spilled — the loss is real and valid. But two cups remain standing behind the figure. The question is when to turn toward what remains. Not denying the grief, but not being consumed by it.
**Reversed:** Beginning to move through grief, slowly acknowledging what remains, acceptance arriving.

### Six of Cups (圣杯六)
**Upright:** Nostalgia, childhood memories, innocence revisited. Reconnecting with the past — often a gentle, healing memory or someone from earlier life returning. Can indicate returning to simpler times or a need to heal old wounds.
**Reversed:** Living too much in the past, nostalgia as avoidance, or an unwillingness to leave an outdated situation.

### Seven of Cups (圣杯七)
**Upright:** Many possibilities, fantasies, illusions. The querent sees many paths but none is yet clear — some are real, some are wishful thinking. Requires discernment. Dreams are vivid but grounding is needed.
**Reversed:** Clarity emerging from confusion, choosing from among many options, cutting through illusion.

### Eight of Cups (圣杯八)
**Upright:** Walking away from something that no longer fulfills, even when it was once good. The eight cups are stacked and complete — nothing is wrong with them — but something is missing. Choosing growth over comfort.
**Reversed:** Refusing to leave something that no longer serves, inability to walk away, or the return to something previously abandoned.

### Nine of Cups (圣杯九)
**Upright:** Satisfaction, contentment, wishes coming true. The "wish card." Emotional fulfillment and self-sufficiency. Things are good — allow the pleasure of that.
**Reversed:** Complacency, greediness, superficial satisfaction that masks deeper lack, or overindulgence.

### Ten of Cups (圣杯十)
**Upright:** Emotional fulfillment and family harmony. The happy ending — not perfect, but genuinely loving and content. Deep relational joy and a sense of belonging.
**Reversed:** Disrupted family harmony, values misalignment within relationships, surface happiness hiding real disconnection.

### Page of Cups (圣杯侍者)
**Upright:** Sensitive, imaginative, emotionally open. May bring unexpected creative or emotional news. A reminder to stay curious and open to what the heart reveals.
**Reversed:** Emotional immaturity, oversensitivity, unrealistic romantic idealism, or messages that don't come.

### Knight of Cups (圣杯骑士)
**Upright:** The romantic, the idealist in pursuit of the dream. Moving toward what is loved with feeling and imagination. May represent an offer or invitation with emotional charge.
**Reversed:** Moodiness, emotional manipulation, unreliable affection, or chasing fantasy over reality.

### Queen of Cups (圣杯王后)
**Upright:** Emotional intelligence mastered. Deeply empathetic, intuitive, and compassionate without losing herself. Holds others' emotions with grace and her own with self-awareness.
**Reversed:** Emotional manipulation, codependency, taking on others' feelings to the point of losing one's own center.

### King of Cups (圣杯国王)
**Upright:** Emotional mastery expressed through wise leadership. Deeply feeling but not governed by feeling — holds space for others' emotional experience with calm authority and compassion.
**Reversed:** Emotional suppression, using empathy manipulatively, or becoming overwhelmed beneath a composed exterior.

## Suit of Swords (宝剑 / 风)

*Domain: thought, communication, conflict, decision, truth, clarity, challenge*

### Ace of Swords (宝剑王牌)
**Upright:** Mental clarity, breakthrough, truth cutting through confusion. A new idea, a decisive moment of understanding, the power to speak or see clearly.
**Reversed:** Confusion, bad information, mental fog, using clarity as cruelty, or a decision made from the wrong premise.

### Two of Swords (宝剑二)
**Upright:** Stalemate, deliberate blindness, an uncomfortable choice being avoided. The figure is blindfolded, swords crossed — acknowledging both sides but refusing to choose. The impasse is only maintained by not looking.
**Reversed:** The blindfold is removed — information arrives that forces a choice. Confusion beginning to clear.

### Three of Swords (宝剑三)
**Upright:** Heartbreak, grief, painful truth, betrayal. The heart is pierced and it hurts — this isn't subtle. Honoring the pain while recognizing that rain passes and healing follows.
**Reversed:** The acute pain is beginning to ease, but full healing isn't yet complete. Old wounds reopened, or grief that is being intellectualized rather than felt.

### Four of Swords (宝剑四)
**Upright:** Rest after battle. Retreat, recovery, the knight laid in the tomb-like rest of convalescence. Necessary stillness after a difficult period. The mind needs to stop fighting.
**Reversed:** Restlessness, returning to action before recovery is complete, forced rest that breeds frustration.

### Five of Swords (宝剑五)
**Upright:** Conflict with a pyrrhic quality — even winning costs something real. Hollow victory, defeating others at the expense of genuine connection or integrity. May also indicate defeat or the aftermath of conflict where no one truly wins.
**Reversed:** Choosing not to fight, reconciliation, letting go of the need to "win" in a way that damages.

### Six of Swords (宝剑六)
**Upright:** Moving away from turbulence toward calmer waters. The journey isn't joyful — the figures sit in silence, carrying baggage — but the transition is happening. Moving toward better, even if not yet arrived.
**Reversed:** Resisting a necessary transition, returning to a troubled situation, or the calm proving illusory.

### Seven of Swords (宝剑七)
**Upright:** Cunning, strategy, operating with less than full information, possible deception. Taking what is needed without confrontation. Can be tactical intelligence or avoidance — context determines which.
**Reversed:** Being exposed, returning what was taken, the consequences of dishonesty arriving, or the querent discovering they've been deceived.

### Eight of Swords (宝剑八)
**Upright:** Self-imposed restriction. The figure is bound and blindfolded, but the ropes are loose and the castle is in the distance — the prison is largely mental. Limiting beliefs are the main chains.
**Reversed:** Releasing mental restrictions, beginning to see the way out, or at times: deeper entrapment as limiting thoughts calcify.

### Nine of Swords (宝剑九)
**Upright:** Anxiety, nightmares, the 3am spiral of fear and regret. The suffering is real, but the swords are on the wall behind the figure — not actually piercing. Often about catastrophizing or being tormented by worst-case thinking.
**Reversed:** Emerging from the darkest fears, beginning to examine anxiety, or deeper despair if the sleeplessness has gone on too long.

### Ten of Swords (宝剑十)
**Upright:** Painful ending, rock bottom, defeat that cannot be worsened. The figure lies with ten swords in the back — it cannot get worse than this. That is actually useful information: the only direction now is up. Sometimes crisis, sometimes the surrender that enables recovery.
**Reversed:** Recovery beginning from the lowest point, gradually rising, or refusing to admit defeat when the ending is necessary.

### Page of Swords (宝剑侍者)
**Upright:** Curious, alert, mentally quick. Gathering information, watching before acting. May bring sharp-edged news or ideas. Loves debate and learning.
**Reversed:** Gossiping, using information unkindly, all talk without action, or mental energy without direction.

### Knight of Swords (宝剑骑士)
**Upright:** Fast, direct, decisive action. Charging forward with conviction — cuts through ambiguity but may not pause long enough to consider consequences. Clarity in motion.
**Reversed:** Recklessness, aggression, cutting words or actions that damage relationships, haste without wisdom.

### Queen of Swords (宝剑王后)
**Upright:** Clear-minded, independent, perceptive. Has experienced difficulty and gained wisdom from it — sees clearly without illusion, communicates directly without cruelty. Her clarity is a gift.
**Reversed:** Coldness, bitterness, using sharp intelligence as a weapon, or emotional wounds calcified into defensive harshness.

### King of Swords (宝剑国王)
**Upright:** Intellectual authority, clear judgment, fair-minded leadership. The mind as master — analytical, honest, impartial. Makes decisions based on principle rather than emotion.
**Reversed:** Tyrannical use of intellectual authority, manipulative intelligence, or cold disregard for emotional reality.

## Suit of Pentacles (星币 / 钱币 / 土)

*Domain: material world, finances, work, body, practical matters, long-term building*

### Ace of Pentacles (星币王牌)
**Upright:** New material opportunity — financial, professional, or practical. A seed of tangible prosperity being offered. Something that, if tended, will grow into real, grounded abundance.
**Reversed:** Missed opportunity, poor financial decision, practical plans that don't materialize.

### Two of Pentacles (星币二)
**Upright:** Juggling multiple priorities with relative ease — for now. Balance in motion, adaptability, managing competing demands. Often appears during busy periods where flexibility is key.
**Reversed:** Overwhelm from too many commitments, poor time/money management, losing control of what was being juggled.

### Three of Pentacles (星币三)
**Upright:** Skilled collaboration, craftsmanship recognized, teamwork producing quality results. Learning through doing, the early stages of mastery, receiving constructive feedback.
**Reversed:** Poor collaboration, work that is mediocre or unappreciated, cutting corners on quality.

### Four of Pentacles (星币四)
**Upright:** Financial stability through holding tight — but the figure clutches coins to chest, feet, and head, immobilized. There is security here, but also rigidity and fear of loss. Context determines: prudent saving or fearful hoarding?
**Reversed:** Releasing financial fear, loosening grip on money or material security, or alternatively: financial instability from poor boundaries.

### Five of Pentacles (星币五)
**Upright:** Material hardship, poverty consciousness, feeling left out in the cold. Two figures pass in the snow outside a lit window — help may be available but they don't look up to see it. Fear of lack, not necessarily actual lack.
**Reversed:** Recovery from financial difficulty, beginning to seek and accept help, hardship easing.

### Six of Pentacles (星币六)
**Upright:** Generosity, giving and receiving, balance of resources. Someone in a position of wealth giving thoughtfully to those in need. May indicate receiving help, giving help, or the balance of exchange in relationships.
**Reversed:** Charity with strings attached, unequal power dynamics, giving to control, or not receiving what is owed.

### Seven of Pentacles (星币七)
**Upright:** Patience in the long-term project. The farmer leans on his hoe, watching what he planted — still waiting for the harvest. Assessment of what has been built; considering whether to continue or pivot.
**Reversed:** Impatience with slow results, questioning whether the investment is worth it, or abandoning something just before it bears fruit.

### Eight of Pentacles (星币八)
**Upright:** Dedicated craft and skill development. The apprentice at work, repeating the task to achieve mastery. Diligent, focused labor — not glamorous, but profoundly productive. Excellence through practice.
**Reversed:** Perfectionism blocking progress, drudgery without growth, poor quality work, or lacking the focus for mastery.

### Nine of Pentacles (星币九)
**Upright:** Earned abundance and self-sufficiency. The figure stands in a garden of her own making — independent, comfortable, having built something real through her own effort. Self-reliance as genuine wealth.
**Reversed:** Financial dependence, hollow material success, someone living beyond their means, or success that required too high a personal cost.

### Ten of Pentacles (星币十)
**Upright:** Generational wealth, family legacy, lasting material security. The fullest expression of earthly abundance — not just personal but woven into family and community. Stability that endures.
**Reversed:** Family conflict over money or inheritance, legacy burdens, or wealth without genuine belonging or meaning.

### Page of Pentacles (星币侍者)
**Upright:** Diligent student of practical skills, beginning a new phase of material development, curious about how the real world works. News about finances, education, or practical opportunities.
**Reversed:** Lack of follow-through on practical plans, unreliable with money, learning not being applied.

### Knight of Pentacles (星币骑士)
**Upright:** Slow, methodical, reliable forward movement. The knight who carries through on every commitment — not flashy, but dependably effective. The value of steady, patient labor.
**Reversed:** Stubbornness, being stuck in a rut, overly cautious to the point of missing opportunities, or conversely: financial recklessness.

### Queen of Pentacles (星币王后)
**Upright:** Nurturing abundance — providing comfort, warmth, and practical care. A deeply grounded, generous presence who makes things grow. Domestic mastery and material wisdom combined with genuine warmth.
**Reversed:** Overemphasis on material security at the expense of connection, smothering rather than nurturing, financial anxiety.

### King of Pentacles (星币国王)
**Upright:** Mastery of the material world through patient, disciplined stewardship. The successful builder, investor, or provider — reliable, generous, and deeply trustworthy with resources. Has built something lasting.
**Reversed:** Financial obsession at the expense of everything else, stubbornness, using money to control, or material success without fulfillment.
