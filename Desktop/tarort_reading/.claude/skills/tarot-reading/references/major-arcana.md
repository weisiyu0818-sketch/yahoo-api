# Major Arcana Reference

The 22 Major Arcana represent universal archetypes and soul-level forces. When multiple Major Arcana appear in a spread, the reading carries heightened significance — archetypal patterns are at work, not just day-to-day circumstances.

---

## 0 — The Fool (愚者 / 愚人)

**Element:** Air | **Keywords (Upright):** New beginnings, spontaneity, innocence, leap of faith, unlimited potential
**Keywords (Reversed):** Recklessness, naivety, poor judgment, hesitation, missed opportunity

**Upright:** The Fool stands at the precipice, about to step into the unknown with joyful trust. This card signals the beginning of a journey — not yet defined, not yet constrained. It calls for open-minded action, unencumbered by past failures or future fears. There is genuine creative potential here if the querent is willing to begin without a guaranteed outcome.

**Reversed:** The energy of new beginnings is blocked — either by excessive caution (refusing to step forward) or by recklessness (leaping without any discernment). Ask whether the hesitation is wisdom or fear, and whether the impulsiveness is freedom or avoidance.

**Combination notes:** The Fool + The World = a completed cycle giving way to a new one. The Fool + The Tower = disruptive change forcing an unexpected fresh start.

## I — The Magician (魔术师)

**Element:** Air/Mercury | **Keywords (Upright):** Willpower, skill, resourcefulness, manifestation, focused action
**Keywords (Reversed):** Manipulation, untapped potential, poor planning, self-doubt, trickery

**Upright:** The Magician has all four elements (Wands, Cups, Swords, Pentacles) at the table and knows how to use them. This card says: the resources exist, the skills are present — now apply focused will. It is the card of "I can make this happen." Particularly strong in career, creative, and practical action spreads.

**Reversed:** Either the skills exist but aren't being used (self-doubt, procrastination), or someone is using their abilities manipulatively. Check whether the querent is underestimating their own capacity, or whether there is deception — from self or others — at play.

**Combination notes:** The Magician + The High Priestess = knowing both action and intuition; balance of doing and receiving. The Magician + The Devil = skill being used in service of ego or compulsion rather than purpose.

---

## II — The High Priestess (女祭司 / 高女祭司)

**Element:** Water/Moon | **Keywords (Upright):** Intuition, mystery, inner knowing, the unconscious, patience
**Keywords (Reversed):** Ignored intuition, secrets withheld, surface-level thinking, disconnection from inner wisdom

**Upright:** The High Priestess does not act — she knows. She sits between two pillars (mystery and revelation) and holds scrolled knowledge in her lap. This card calls for stillness, inner listening, and trusting what isn't yet fully visible. The answer exists, but it requires turning inward rather than seeking external validation.

**Reversed:** Something important is being overlooked — either gut feelings that are being dismissed, or information that is deliberately being kept hidden. It can signal self-deception or a refusal to look at uncomfortable truths.

**Combination notes:** The High Priestess + The Moon = deep unconscious material surfacing; dreams and intuition as guidance. The High Priestess + The Emperor = tension between feeling/knowing and logic/structure.

---

## III — The Empress (女皇)

**Element:** Earth/Venus | **Keywords (Upright):** Abundance, nurturing, creativity, fertility, sensory pleasure, growth
**Keywords (Reversed):** Creative block, codependency, neglecting self, smothering, stagnation

**Upright:** The Empress is the energy of creation in full bloom — the fertile ground where things can grow. She governs not just literal fertility but all forms of creative manifestation, nurturing relationships, and abundance mindset. In practical spreads: growth is supported; allow things to develop naturally without forcing.

**Reversed:** Creative energy is blocked or misdirected. This can indicate burnout from over-giving, codependent caretaking, or a failure to tend to one's own needs. Something that should be growing has stalled.

**Combination notes:** The Empress + The Emperor = balanced creation: vision meets structure. The Empress + The Hermit = a need to step back from nurturing others to tend the inner life.

---

## IV — The Emperor (皇帝)

**Element:** Fire/Aries | **Keywords (Upright):** Authority, structure, stability, fathering energy, logic, long-term strategy
**Keywords (Reversed):** Rigidity, domination, lack of control, authoritarianism, emotional unavailability

**Upright:** The Emperor establishes order and builds lasting structures. This card signals a time for strategic thinking, setting clear boundaries, and taking responsibility with discipline. In relationship spreads, it can represent a stable or paternal figure; in work spreads, it suggests leadership and organization are key.

**Reversed:** Control has tipped into rigidity or domination. Structure that once helped has become a cage. Can also indicate someone avoiding responsibility by overclaiming authority, or someone who is emotionally shut down beneath a controlled exterior.

**Combination notes:** The Emperor + The Hierophant = deep institutional/conventional authority. The Emperor + The Tower = a system or authority structure about to be dismantled.

---

## V — The Hierophant (教皇 / 祭司长)

**Element:** Earth/Taurus | **Keywords (Upright):** Tradition, institutions, spiritual guidance, mentorship, shared beliefs
**Keywords (Reversed):** Dogma, rebellion against norms, unconventional path, hypocrisy, rigid beliefs

**Upright:** The Hierophant connects individual experience to collective wisdom — traditions, institutions, teachers, and established systems. This card asks: what does established guidance offer here? It may point to seeking a mentor, honoring a commitment, or working within a structure that has proven value.

**Reversed:** The conventional path is being questioned — or should be. This is not necessarily negative; sometimes the established way no longer fits. Can also flag hypocrisy in authority figures or self-imposed dogma blocking growth.

**Combination notes:** The Hierophant + The Lovers = a conventionally significant commitment (marriage, formal partnership). The Hierophant + The Fool = choosing between the traditional path and a leap into the unknown.

---

## VI — The Lovers (恋人)

**Element:** Air/Gemini | **Keywords (Upright):** Choice, alignment of values, partnership, harmony, authentic connection
**Keywords (Reversed):** Misaligned values, poor choices, inner conflict, broken commitment, codependency

**Upright:** Despite the name, The Lovers is fundamentally a card about **choice** — specifically, aligning choice with one's deepest values. In relationships: genuine connection built on shared values. In career/life decisions: the need to choose with the whole self, not just the practical mind. What would you choose if you were fully honest?

**Reversed:** A choice is being made that conflicts with true values, or the querent is avoiding a necessary decision. Can indicate a relationship where the connection is pleasant but the foundation is misaligned.

**Combination notes:** The Lovers + The Two of Cups = deep romantic/soulmate resonance. The Lovers + The Devil = attachment that feels like love but operates through compulsion or fear.

---

## VII — The Chariot (战车)

**Element:** Water/Cancer | **Keywords (Upright):** Victory, willpower, control, determination, forward momentum
**Keywords (Reversed):** Loss of control, scattered direction, aggression, obstacles, forcing outcomes

**Upright:** The Chariot represents mastering opposing forces to achieve movement. The driver doesn't fight the two sphinxes — they harness them. This is discipline and determination applied with skill. Strong card for overcoming obstacles, completing projects, and making decisive forward progress.

**Reversed:** Momentum has stalled or is being forced in the wrong direction. May indicate trying to control something uncontrollable, or channeling determination into conflict and aggression rather than progress.

**Combination notes:** The Chariot + Strength = unstoppable determination. The Chariot + The Moon = uncertain direction; moving forward without being able to see clearly.

---

## VIII — Strength (力量)

**Element:** Fire/Leo | **Keywords (Upright):** Inner strength, patience, compassion, courage, taming impulse
**Keywords (Reversed):** Self-doubt, weakness, fear, raw emotion overwhelming reason, repression

**Upright:** Strength is not about force — it is about the quiet, patient power of compassion over aggression. The figure tames the lion not by fighting it, but by meeting it with calm presence. This card suggests that the querent has more inner resources than they realize, and that patience and gentleness will succeed where force would fail.

**Reversed:** Inner strength is being eroded — either by excessive self-criticism, by suppressing emotions until they become overwhelming, or by allowing fear to dominate. The lion is winning.

**Combination notes:** Strength + The Hermit = deep inner work; strength that comes from solitude and self-knowledge. Strength + The Tower = inner resilience being tested by external collapse.

---

## IX — The Hermit (隐士)

**Element:** Earth/Virgo | **Keywords (Upright):** Solitude, inner wisdom, introspection, guidance, stepping back
**Keywords (Reversed):** Isolation, loneliness, withdrawal, stubborn refusal, rejecting help

**Upright:** The Hermit withdraws not from life, but toward wisdom. Holding a lantern aloft, the figure guides — both self and others — through inner light. This card calls for deliberate solitude, deep reflection, and turning away from external noise to hear what is true. Often appears when a period of quiet introspection is needed before the next move.

**Reversed:** Isolation has become harmful — either chosen as avoidance of connection, or imposed and painful. The hermit energy, once healthy, has curdled into disconnection or stubbornness.

**Combination notes:** The Hermit + The Star = emerging from retreat with renewed hope. The Hermit + The Six of Cups = reconnecting with the past during a period of reflection.

---

## X — Wheel of Fortune (命运之轮)

**Element:** Fire/Jupiter | **Keywords (Upright):** Cycles, turning points, fate, luck, momentum, inevitable change
**Keywords (Reversed):** Resistance to change, bad luck, disrupted cycles, clinging to what was

**Upright:** The Wheel turns — always. This card marks a turning point, a shift in fortune, or the recognition that a cycle is completing and another beginning. It carries fateful, larger-than-personal energy. Often appears when circumstances are about to shift in ways the querent can ride but not fully control.

**Reversed:** A cycle that should be completing is being resisted, or change is happening in a destabilizing way. May indicate being caught in repetitive loops without recognizing them.

**Combination notes:** Wheel of Fortune + The World = a major cycle completing with full integration. Wheel of Fortune + The Moon = uncertainty about which direction the wheel is turning; trust the process.

---

## XI — Justice (正义)

**Element:** Air/Libra | **Keywords (Upright):** Fairness, truth, cause and effect, accountability, legal matters
**Keywords (Reversed):** Injustice, dishonesty, evasion of responsibility, biased judgment

**Upright:** Justice sees clearly and weighs without favoritism. This card indicates that outcomes will be proportional to actions taken — what was done will have its corresponding consequence, positive or negative. In practical spreads: legal or contractual matters, accountability, or a moment of truth-reckoning.

**Reversed:** Something fair is being suppressed — either the querent is avoiding accountability, or they are experiencing genuine injustice. The scales are not balanced.

**Combination notes:** Justice + The High Priestess = truth that is hidden but will eventually surface. Justice + The Devil = karmic accounting for choices made under compulsion or self-deception.

---

## XII — The Hanged Man (倒吊人)

**Element:** Water/Neptune | **Keywords (Upright):** Surrender, new perspective, pause, sacrifice, letting go of control
**Keywords (Reversed):** Stalling, martyrdom, useless sacrifice, refusing to surrender, indecision

**Upright:** The Hanged Man is suspended voluntarily — not a victim, but someone who has chosen to pause and shift perspective. He sees the world differently from this angle, and that shift in view is the gift. This card calls for voluntary surrender of a rigid approach, a willingness to do nothing while something completes itself, and openness to seeing an entirely different angle.

**Reversed:** The pause has become stalling, or the querent is suffering unnecessarily without gaining the insight that the hanging was meant to provide. Can indicate martyrdom — sacrifice without transformation.

**Combination notes:** The Hanged Man + The Moon = profound disorientation that, when released, yields deep understanding. The Hanged Man + The Wheel of Fortune = necessary suspension before a major cycle turn.

---

## XIII — Death (死神)

**Element:** Water/Scorpio | **Keywords (Upright):** Transformation, endings, transition, letting go, metamorphosis
**Keywords (Reversed):** Resistance to change, stagnation, clinging, inability to complete a transition

**Upright:** Death is almost never literal in tarot. It is the card of radical transformation — the ending of one form so another can begin. Like the caterpillar's dissolution, what is lost is real, but what emerges could not exist without that dissolution. This card appears when something must genuinely end for growth to occur: a relationship, an identity, a belief, a chapter.

**Reversed:** The ending that needs to happen is being resisted, stalling the transformation. The querent may be clinging to something that no longer serves — a relationship, a role, a story about themselves.

**Combination notes:** Death + The Tower = sudden, forced transformation rather than chosen release. Death + Judgement = transformation that leads to awakening and new calling.

---

## XIV — Temperance (节制)

**Element:** Fire/Sagittarius | **Keywords (Upright):** Balance, patience, integration, moderation, alchemy, long view
**Keywords (Reversed):** Imbalance, excess, impatience, misalignment, failed integration

**Upright:** Temperance blends — patiently, carefully, continuously. The angel pours water between two cups, mixing without spilling. This is the card of the long game: integration of opposites, sustainable balance, and trust in a gradual process that is working even when it isn't visibly dramatic. Often appears when something important is being synthesized or when patience is required.

**Reversed:** Things are out of balance. Excess in one direction has destabilized the whole. The querent may be pushing too hard in one area, or trying to rush an integration that requires time.

**Combination notes:** Temperance + The Star = healing after difficulty; gradual restoration. Temperance + The Chariot = needing to balance determination with patience.

---

## XV — The Devil (恶魔)

**Element:** Earth/Capricorn | **Keywords (Upright):** Bondage, materialism, shadow, addiction, compulsion, trapped thinking
**Keywords (Reversed):** Breaking free, reclaiming power, releasing addiction, beginning to question chains

**Upright:** The chains on the Devil's figures are loose — they could step free, but they don't. This is the card of self-imposed bondage: compulsions, addictions, beliefs that trap, material over-attachment, or relationships powered by fear and need rather than love. The Devil reveals what holds the querent in place and asks: why are you still here?

**Reversed:** The querent is beginning to see through the illusion and loosen the chains. A pattern of bondage is being recognized and questioned. Freedom is closer than it appears — but requires honest examination.

**Combination notes:** The Devil + The Lovers = a relationship or choice with deep compulsive attachment. The Devil + Strength = the inner battle between impulse and self-mastery.

---

## XVI — The Tower (塔)

**Element:** Fire/Mars | **Keywords (Upright):** Sudden disruption, revelation, collapse of false structures, liberation through destruction
**Keywords (Reversed):** Narrowly avoided disaster, prolonged disruption, internal upheaval, resistance to necessary breakdown

**Upright:** Lightning strikes what was built on a faulty foundation. The Tower does not fall because it was weak — it falls because it was wrong. The disruption is real and often painful, but it clears what needed clearing. In the rubble, something more honest can be built. Appears when sudden change, shocking revelation, or collapse of a belief/relationship/structure is either happening or necessary.

**Reversed:** The collapse is slower, more internal, or being delayed. The shock may come from within rather than outside. The querent may be resisting an upheaval that has already begun.

**Combination notes:** The Tower + Death = profound, irreversible transformation. The Tower + The Star = destruction followed by healing and renewed hope.

---

## XVII — The Star (星星)

**Element:** Air/Aquarius | **Keywords (Upright):** Hope, renewal, inspiration, healing, faith, serenity after storm
**Keywords (Reversed):** Despair, loss of faith, hopelessness, blocked inspiration, feeling abandoned

**Upright:** After the Tower's collapse, The Star appears. She pours water steadily onto land and into the pool — replenishing, restoring. This is the card of renewed faith, gentle healing, and the quiet certainty that things are moving in the right direction even if slowly. Particularly potent when the querent has been through a difficult period and is beginning to find their footing again.

**Reversed:** Hope has dimmed — the querent may be struggling to believe in a better outcome, or inspiration has dried up. This reversed position calls for gentle self-compassion rather than pressure to "be optimistic."

**Combination notes:** The Star + Temperance = patient, sustainable healing. The Star + The Sun = recovery leading to full vitality and joy.

---

## XVIII — The Moon (月亮)

**Element:** Water/Pisces | **Keywords (Upright):** Illusion, unconscious fears, intuition, the unknown, confusion, depth
**Keywords (Reversed):** Releasing fear, emerging clarity, repressed material surfacing

**Upright:** The Moon illuminates without clarity — it shows and distorts simultaneously. This is the card of dreams, the unconscious, fears that feel real but may not be, and the territory where things aren't what they seem. When The Moon appears, pay attention to what feelings and images arise that resist rational explanation. Something below the surface needs acknowledgment.

**Reversed:** The fog is beginning to lift. Repressed fears or unconscious patterns are rising to consciousness where they can be examined. This is often a positive shift even if the surfacing material is uncomfortable.

**Combination notes:** The Moon + The High Priestess = deep intuitive wisdom to be trusted even without logical explanation. The Moon + The Star = moving through confusion toward renewal.

---

## XIX — The Sun (太阳)

**Element:** Fire/Sun | **Keywords (Upright):** Joy, vitality, clarity, success, optimism, childlike delight
**Keywords (Reversed):** Temporary setback, dimmed joy, unrealistic optimism, blocked vitality

**Upright:** The Sun is unambiguously joyful — clarity, warmth, success, and the pure pleasure of being alive. In readings, it is a genuinely positive sign: things are going well, or they are moving toward resolution and celebration. It can also point toward childlike openness — approaching something with fresh eyes and wholehearted enthusiasm.

**Reversed:** The joy is real but temporary, or slightly out of reach. May indicate overconfidence, or a period where the querent must work to maintain positivity against real challenges.

**Combination notes:** The Sun + The World = complete fulfillment and achievement. The Sun + The Tower = success emerging from, or about to face, disruption.

---

## XX — Judgement (审判)

**Element:** Fire/Pluto | **Keywords (Upright):** Awakening, calling, reckoning, absolution, rebirth, inner evaluation
**Keywords (Reversed):** Self-doubt, ignoring a calling, harsh self-judgment, inability to forgive

**Upright:** Judgement is the great awakening — figures rise from coffins in response to the angel's call. This is the card of hearing a calling clearly and rising to meet it. It may signal a moment of reckoning (being honest about the past), absolution (releasing old guilt), or a clear shift in life direction as something deep within becomes impossible to ignore.

**Reversed:** The calling is being heard but not answered — fear, self-doubt, or excessive self-criticism is preventing the querent from rising to their own potential. Can also indicate harsh self-judgment standing in the way of genuine integration.

**Combination notes:** Judgement + Death = profound transformation leading to a new calling. Judgement + The World = completing a full cycle and awakening to a new level of self.

---

## XXI — The World (世界)

**Element:** Earth/Saturn | **Keywords (Upright):** Completion, integration, achievement, wholeness, cycle fulfilled
**Keywords (Reversed):** Incomplete ending, delayed completion, not fully integrating the lesson

**Upright:** The World is the final card of the Major Arcana — the dancer at the center of the wreath, holding both wands, all four fixed signs present at the corners. This is completion in the fullest sense: not just finishing, but integrating everything that was learned along the way. A chapter closes with genuine wholeness. In future spreads: the completion you're moving toward is real and within reach.

**Reversed:** The ending is near but not quite complete — something hasn't been fully integrated or acknowledged. The querent may be rushing to declare completion before the work is done, or avoiding the final step.

**Combination notes:** The World + The Fool = completion giving rise to a completely new beginning. The World + Judgement = the journey is over; a new calling is beginning.
